1. Sadhak Sanskar Parishad
A. Office Management Department 
The Office Management Department ensures smooth administration, clear processes, and accountable systems, allowing every council and team to work efficiently and stay connected with Gurukarya. It supports reporting, documentation, dispute resolution, communication desks, and coordination across Dhyansthalis. 
Points of concern
Processes are not uniform across Dhyansthalis.
Reporting and documentation sometimes get delayed.
Disputes lack a standard handling mechanism.
Communication systems (calls, records, follow-ups) are inconsistent.
Solutions: implemented & planned
Creating clear SOPs for offerings, MOUs, and disputes.
Digitising volunteer records with defined roles.
Establishing call-handling desks and logs at all Dhyansthalis.
Monthly coordination meetings for alignment and accountability.
Objectives + Planning
100% documentation, receipts, and reporting within set timelines.
Standardised dispute-resolution workflow with full records.
Fully functional call desks across Dhyansthalis with response benchmarks.
Digitised volunteer database and regular orientation sessions.
Consistent council coordination and approved MOU systems.
B. Network Development Department 
The Network Department builds meaningful relationships with corporates, institutions, NGOs, and professionals. It promotes meditation centres, counselling support, collaborations, and workplace wellbeing programs through MOUs and partnerships.
Points of concern
Corporate meditation centres are still limited.
Corporate teams require structured support to maintain regular meditation practice.
Solutions: implemented & planned
Encourage more meditation centres inside corporate offices.
Invite corporate groups to Dhyansthalis for day-retreats and exposure.
Train corporate instructors and create counselling/workshop offerings.
Expand collaborations and MOUs with institutions.
Build authorised volunteer teams to handle sessions professionally.
Objectives + Planning
Develop corporate meditation centres across states and metros.
Create a national pool of trained corporate instructors.
Offer regular counselling and stress-free workshops to companies.
Strengthen expansion, collaboration, and formal MOUs.
Build state-level networking teams. 
C. Sadhak Seva Helpline Department 
The Helpline offers compassionate listening, guidance, and verification support for seekers through calls, chats, WhatsApp, and follow-ups, ensuring clarity, care, and emotional reassurance.
Points of concern
Seekers often feel unsure of whom to contact.
Information is scattered across channels.
Follow-ups and FAQs are not always documented.
Solutions: implemented & planned
Dedicated 24×7 helpline channels.
Structured follow-up system for queries.
Content creation: FAQs, messages, experiences, and guidance archives.
Objectives + Planning
Centralised FAQ and inquiry system on the website.
Daily messages in major languages.
Continuous training classes for volunteers.
Birthday greetings and seeker-relationship system.
Collect and publish authentic experiences and guidance.
Build a strong blogging and updates team. 
D. Sadhak Sanskar Department 
This department nurtures holistic spiritual growth across all ages through discipline, training, values, counselling, life-stage programs, and meditation-aligned education.
Points of concern
Different centres follow different formats.
Many seekers need clearer guidance at life stages.
Training resources are not uniformly available.
Solutions: implemented & planned
Standard training modules and interviews for centre leaders.
Myth-busting, value-based content and exhibitions.
Focus programs: Garbhadhan Sanskar, Bal Sanskar, Youth, Marriage, and Senior Well-being.
Structured workshops, counselling, and expert sessions.
Objectives + Planning

Bring uniformity across programs and centres.
Create expert teams and regular training (online/offline).
Organise child and youth camps.
Strengthen pre-marriage and post-marriage counselling.
Develop meditation modules for schools and colleges.
E. Centre Development Department 
This team supports formation, smooth running, and harmony across meditation centres from operations and feedback to collective meditations and camps.
Points of concern
New centres require guidance and consistency.
Issues sometimes go unresolved or unreported.
Large events need streamlined coordination.
Solutions: implemented & planned
Monitor networks to support 5–20 centres per volunteer.
Feedback systems and R&D inputs.
Activity, logistics, and corporate networking teams.
Structured planning for Purnima/Sunday meditations and Shibirs.
Objectives + Planning
Develop more home centres, especially clusters in major cities.
Uniform training and material for all centres.
1000 home centres in Gandhinagar.
Support centre development abroad via country heads.
Build state-level centre teams.
